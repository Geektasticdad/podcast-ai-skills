# Standalone Episode Narrative Builder

This skill takes a completed standalone episode outline and writes the full spoken narrative for every section. The output is formatted for Microsoft Word — ready to open, review, and record from.

This is a standalone episode narrative. There is no series recap section.

This is a single-input skill. Paste the outline and the full narrative is produced. No additional questions.

**Requires:** Load the Research Foundation skill (`foundation/SKILL.md`) alongside this skill before you begin.

---

## Input

"Paste the full output from your `podcast-episode-outline` for the episode you want to write."

Once the outline is pasted, produce the complete narrative document without asking further questions.

---

## Output Format

Format the entire output for Microsoft Word. Use clear headings for each section, leave space between sections, and label word counts at the end of each section so Jason can check length at a glance. The document should be ready to read aloud without any cleanup.

All scripture references must use the NIV translation.

---

## Section 1: Introduction

**Length:** 260–390 words (approximately 2–3 minutes spoken)

**Structure to Follow:**

1. Hook the Listener
   - Begin with a compelling question, thought, or vivid statement that grabs attention.
   - Connect the listener emotionally to the topic of the episode.
   - Make the listener curious about what they will learn.

2. Preview the Episode Themes
   - Briefly summarize the key ideas that will be explored in the episode.
   - Explain why this topic matters to everyday Christians.
   - Create anticipation for the deeper discussion ahead.

---

## Section 2: Topic Introduction

**Length:** 700–1,200 words (approximately 5–10 minutes spoken)

**Structure to Follow:**

1. Introduce the Topic Clearly
   - Transition naturally from the Introduction.
   - Explain what the topic is and why it is important to understand.
   - Frame the main question or idea the episode will explore.

2. Provide Context for the Topic
   - Explain the biblical or theological context of the topic.
   - Describe the situation, teachings, or events surrounding the subject.
   - Help listeners understand what was happening in Scripture when this topic arises.

3. Cultural or Historical Background
   - Provide historical or cultural insights that help modern listeners understand the passage or topic.
   - Explain relevant practices, beliefs, or societal norms from the time.
   - Use insights consistent with respected biblical commentaries.

4. Why This Topic Matters Today
   - Connect the ancient biblical context to modern Christian life.
   - Explain why this topic is still deeply relevant for believers today.
   - Provide practical examples that listeners can relate to.

---

## Section 3: Topic Discussion

**Length:** Scale to the time estimate provided in the outline for each subsection.

Write one narrative segment per subsection from the outline. Label each subsection clearly. Work through every subsection in order before moving to the Conclusion.

**Structure to Follow (for each subsection):**

1. Introduce the Subtopic
   - Begin by clearly introducing the key idea being discussed in this section.
   - Connect it naturally to the overall episode theme.
   - Briefly explain what listeners will learn in this segment.

2. Walk Through the Key Talking Points
   - Expand on the talking points from the outline in a clear, logical order.
   - Explain theological ideas in a way that is understandable to new believers.
   - Use relatable explanations or examples when helpful.

3. Explore the Supporting Scriptures
   - Introduce each Bible passage naturally within the teaching.
   - Quote the verse in the NIV translation.
   - Explain what the passage teaches and how it relates to the topic.

4. Include Commentary Insights
   - Integrate insights from trusted biblical commentaries when explaining the passage.
   - Provide historical context, theological depth, or clarification of difficult ideas.
   - Keep explanations accessible and conversational rather than academic.

5. Connect to Real-World Application
   - Explain how the biblical truth applies to everyday Christian life.
   - Provide practical examples or questions listeners can reflect on.
   - Encourage listeners to consider how the teaching might impact their faith, choices, or perspective.

---

## Section 4: Conclusion

**Length:** 150–300 words (approximately 1–2 minutes spoken)

**Structure to Follow:**

1. Summarize the Key Lessons
   - Briefly recap the most important ideas discussed in the episode.
   - Reinforce the central truth the listener should remember.

2. Reinforce the Listener Takeaway
   - Clearly restate the core message of the episode.
   - Emphasize why this truth matters for the listener's faith and walk with Christ.

3. Encourage Practical Action
   - Encourage listeners to apply the teaching in their everyday lives.
   - Suggest simple ways they might live out what they learned.
   - Inspire reflection, prayer, or action.

4. Optional Closing Scripture
   - If provided in the outline, introduce and quote the closing verse using the NIV translation.
   - Briefly connect the verse to the episode's message.

5. End with Encouragement
   - Close with an uplifting statement that encourages listeners in their faith journey.
   - The ending should feel natural for a podcast and leave listeners feeling encouraged and hopeful.

---

## Next Step

Once the narrative is reviewed and approved, the episode is ready for recording.
